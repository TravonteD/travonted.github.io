I'm an avid user of the terminal and the shell. My journey started out with bash and I used it for a long time, then I took the (seemingly common) path of moving over to zsh. I've been using zsh now for about 6 years, and I'm curious about a couple of new ideas that have been added to the shell ecosystem.

I still plan on writing all of my script in POSIX complaint shell, since I sometimes share them with others. This exploration of shells is more so for my day-to-day stringing together of various commands to get things done. The shells that I'm planning on trying out are

- [Oil](https://www.oilshell.org/)
- [Nushell](https://www.nushell.sh/)
- [Elvish Shell](https://elv.sh/)

Each of these shells seem to offer more flexibility and power when piping commands, and perhaps could save me some time on the commandline. The plan is to spend a week with each one as my primary shell. That will give me insight into what it's like to configure and use it in my own personal uses, then I'll write about it here! Stay tuned if you're interested.
